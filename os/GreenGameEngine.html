About

While research has explored the economic and social impacts of gaming, its energy consumption remains largely overlooked. Video game engines like Unreal, Unity, and Godot offer little insight into their energy costs. Our recent research work revealed that Unreal’s Blueprints consume significantly more energy than traditional coding—an overlooked inefficiency. The financial support offered by the Epic MegaGrant would enable us to dive deeper into the energy consumption and optimisation for Unreal. We plan to measure consumptions at API level and to build analytics insights to support developers in making greener design and implementation choices. We will also measure the consumption of other engines for benchmarking purposes. The ultimate project's outcome is to position Unreal as the leading “green” engine. Our findings will help developers make decision that result into more energy-efficient engines. This research has broader societal implications, promoting sustainability in a rapidly growing industry. Additionally, we hope to raise awareness and encourage more researchers, developers, and educators to explore energy efficiency in gaming. By shedding light on this overlooked issue, we can drive meaningful change for both the industry and the environment.
